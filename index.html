Dr. Elizabeth Johnston

Environmental Systems Scientist | Climate Resilience | Data-Driven Policy

About

I am a systems scientist working at the intersection of environmental change, data science, and public policy. My research focuses on landscape dynamics, climate resilience, and the use of high-performance computing for geospatial analysis.

Research

  • Post-Fire Landslide Susceptibility: Modeling slope failure risk using remote sensing and precipitation forecasts.
  • Early Warning Systems: Evaluating the performance of climate-related disaster alerts.
  • Resilience Metrics: Developing spatial indicators to inform pre-disaster planning.

Selected Publications

  • Johnston, E. (2024). *Landscape recovery and predictive risk after wildfire*. Journal of Environmental Systems.
  • Johnston, E., et al. (2023). *Forecasting landslide risk using climate data*. Remote Sensing Letters.
